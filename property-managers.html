What you get

Six things a one-off service call doesn't give you.

A direct dispatcher.

Skip the front line. Your dispatcher knows your portfolio, your buildings, and which problems have happened before. One phone tree, not seven.

Priority response.

90-minute average arrival for portfolio sites with an active backup, citywide, 24/7. Non-emergencies get a two-hour booking window the next business day.

A drain map per building.

We camera every main lateral once at onboarding and file the footage. Next call, the tech already knows where the cleanout is and which joint is bellied at 32 feet.

Tenant-facing paperwork.

A one-page notice we leave on the unit door (in English and French if you want), an invoice the tenant can read, and a 24-hour follow-up email confirming the line is clear.

Consolidated billing.

One monthly statement, line-itemized by building and unit. Pay by EFT or credit card. NET 30 standard, NET 45 for high-volume accounts.

Footage you can keep.

Every camera run, every job, sent as an MP4 with date, building, address, and footage-distance markers. Useful for insurance claims, board meetings, and roots-vs.-roots arguments.

Response SLA

Four tiers. Written response times. No "we'll try."

Portfolio accounts get the same dispatch matrix every time. The tier is set when the call comes in — by the dispatcher, not the technician — and the time-on-site target is locked from that moment. If we miss the SLA, the call-out fee is waived. We've waived it three times in 2025.

Tenant-ready paperwork

Three pieces of paper. Every job. Every time.

A backup in unit 514 generates roughly 18 emails between a tenant, a manager, an owner, an insurer, and a vendor. We replace most of that with a small packet — written so the tenant doesn't have to ask follow-up questions and the building owner doesn't have to interpret what we did.

  • Door notice.

    Left on the unit's door before we leave. Plain English, what we ran, when to use the drain again, our number for follow-up.

  • Itemized work order.

    Sent to the manager within 60 minutes of leaving site. Time-on-site, method (cable / jet / camera), length cleared, parts used, photo of the cleanout cap going back on.

  • Camera video, if run.

    MP4 with footage-distance markers, the building address in the file name, and a thumbnail of the worst spot. Filed in the building's drain folder so you can pull it next year.

Property manager FAQ

Questions managers ask before signing on.

Do we have to sign a contract?
No. There's a one-page agreement that confirms the SLA tiers, who's authorized to open calls on each building, and the billing terms (NET 30 standard). It's open-ended — either side can end the arrangement with 30 days' notice. We don't sell prepaid hours and we won't lock you into a multi-year deal.
How do you bill — per building, per event, per month?
Per event. One monthly statement aggregates every service event across your portfolio, line-itemized by building and unit. EFT or credit card. We can also bill the corporation, the management company, or the unit owner directly — set per building during onboarding.
Can the tenant call you directly?
Yes — if you set it up that way. Each building has a list of authorized callers (manager, resident manager, sometimes tenants for emergencies only). When the tenant calls, dispatch confirms the building, opens a ticket against your account, and copies you on the work order within an hour. No surprises on the monthly statement.
What happens after-hours? Is there a different number?
Same number. (204) 786-4060 rolls to the on-call dispatcher between 11 pm and 7 am. P1 emergency calls are dispatched immediately, no surcharge. P2 calls are confirmed for first thing the next morning with a phone call (not a voicemail) by 8 am.
Do you carry insurance? Can you name us as additional insured?
Yes. We carry commercial general liability, auto, and WCB Manitoba coverage. We can name your management company or any specific corporation as additional insured on the COI. Send the request through your dispatcher and we'll issue it within two business days.
What if you can't make the SLA?
The call-out fee for that visit is waived. We also send a written note to the building file explaining what happened. We've missed the P1 SLA three times in 2025 — twice during the April thaw flood, once during a wind storm that closed half the city. We don't make a habit of it.
Will your tech show ID and respect tenant access?
Yes. Photo ID, WSD-branded uniform, and a 'today's tech' email sent to the manager before the visit with name and photo. We knock before entering a unit even with manager-provided keys, and we don't enter occupied units unless a tenant is home or you've explicitly authorized entry.
Can you do work outside of sewer and drain — e.g. fix a leaking faucet while you're there?
No. Sewer and drain only. If you need general plumbing we'll recommend two shops we've worked alongside on shared jobs. We'd rather refer than half-do something outside our lane.
What about condo corporations — who signs?
The corporation does, normally through the property manager acting on the board's behalf. We're happy to attend one board meeting during onboarding to walk through the SLA and the drain-map process if it helps the board sign off.
