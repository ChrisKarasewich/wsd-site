When you need it

Signs you've got a sewer line, not a drain, problem.

Sewer line problems show up across the house at the same time. The floor drain gurgles when the toilet flushes. The basement shower backs up when the kitchen sink runs. The lowest fixture in the home is usually the first to overflow.

That's the lateral — the single buried pipe carrying every drain in your house out to the city main. If it's restricted, everything upstream backs up. A snake from the kitchen sink won't reach it; this is a different job, with a different tool.

Anatomy

What we're actually cleaning.

CABLE FROM THE CLEANOUT

We feed a sectional cable through the home's main cleanout — not a kitchen sink — and run it the full length of the lateral.

OR JET FROM THE CLEANOUT

Hydro-jet washes the pipe wall instead of just punching a hole. Better on grease, scale, and soft root mats.

Before we leave, we run the camera the full length and email you the footage. You see the line yourself.

Method

Three tools. Our camera picks which one we use.

Mainline cable

Roots and hard blockages.

A 3/4-inch sectional cable with a root-cutter head. Designed to chew through root masses in 4-inch clay tile that a drain cable would just snap on.

Hydro-jet

Grease, scale, soft roots.

High-pressure water (up to 4,000 PSI) scours the pipe wall instead of just opening a channel. The line comes out closer to its original 4-inch diameter.

Locate

Broken pipe or belly.

If the line is collapsed, offset, or sagging, cleaning won't help. The camera locator marks the spot on your lawn to within 12 inches. That becomes the spot dig.

What we actually find down there

Five things we pull out of Winnipeg sewer lines every week.

01

Roots in clay tile.

Older Winnipeg neighborhoods (Wolseley, the West End, Crescentwood, parts of North End and St. Boniface) sit on clay tile sewer pipe. Joints leak a little water. Roots find them. Over a decade they grow inside the line and choke it.

02

Grease and scale.

Decades of cooking grease, soap, and mineral buildup narrowing a 4-inch pipe to less than 2 inches of clear opening. Common in older kitchens and in any building with a long lateral.

03

Broken pipe.

Clay tile cracks. Cast iron rusts through from the inside. PVC gets crushed when soil shifts. Once the pipe is broken, water leaves the line and debris collects in the gap.

04

Bellies.

The pipe sags from settling soil. Water pools in the low spot. Debris settles into the standing water. Cable a belly all you want; it'll be back next quarter. The camera locates it and the repair is a spot dig.

05

Frozen line.

Winnipeg clay tile under shallow yards and driveway crossings freezes January through March when standing water sits in the line overnight. That's a different page.

Sewer line FAQ

Questions on this service.

My toilet, tub, and floor drain all backed up at once. What's going on?
That's a textbook lateral restriction. Everything in the house drains through one pipe to the city main; when that pipe is choked, the lowest fixtures back up first. Don't run more water — call us and we'll camera the line and clear it.
Do I own the lateral or does the city?
In Winnipeg, the homeowner owns the lateral from the house all the way to the city main connection — including the section under the boulevard and street. If the problem is past the main, that's on the city. Our camera tells you exactly where the issue sits.
How often should a sewer line be cleaned?
If you've got mature trees over a clay tile lateral — every 12–24 months as preventative. Newer PVC laterals with no root pressure may only need a clean when something changes. We'll tell you on the first visit which camp you're in.
Will cabling damage clay tile?
Modern sectional cable with the right head is safe on intact clay tile. The risk is when the tile is already broken — cable can catch the offset and worsen it. That's why we run a camera before cabling. If the line is compromised, we jet at lower pressure or skip straight to a repair plan.
What if the camera finds broken pipe?
We stop and show you the footage on screen. You'll get three written options — spot repair, trenchless liner, or full replacement — with prices, before anyone digs. The camera fee applies to whichever repair you choose.
Can you clear the line through a toilet or sink instead of a cleanout?
We can, but we don't if we can help it. A proper main cleanout gives us straight-line access to the lateral. Pulling a toilet adds time and a reset fee. If you don't have a cleanout, we'll quote installing one — it's worth it the second time the line clogs.
How long does a typical sewer line cleaning take?
Camera plus cable, one to three hours. Camera plus jet, two to four hours. If we find broken pipe, the cleaning stops and the conversation turns to repair planning.
