What we treat as an emergency

If it can’t wait for morning, neither do we.

Sewer and drain only — that’s the whole job. If it’s one of these, call now. If you’re not sure, call anyway and we’ll tell you straight whether it can wait.

Active sewer backup

Sewage coming up through a floor drain, tub, or basement. Stop running water and call — this is the one we drop everything for.

Overflowing toilet

Toilet won't flush or is overflowing and the plunger isn't doing it. Usually a blockage further down the line.

Frozen sewer line

Every drain in the house dead at once in deep winter. Steam-thaw, safe on clay, cast iron, and PVC.

Flooded basement

Water rising from a floor drain or backing up faster than it leaves. We clear the line and get it draining.

Burst or leaking drain pipe

A cracked or leaking drain or sewer pipe dumping water where it shouldn't be. We locate it and stop the loss.

Commercial shut-down risk

A restaurant or building that can't open with a blocked line. Priority dispatch so you're back in service fast.

While you wait for us

Four things to do the moment you hang up.

None of this fixes the line — that’s our job. It just keeps a bad situation from getting worse before we arrive.

Stop running water

No sinks, showers, laundry, or dishwasher. Every drop you add has nowhere to go and makes the backup worse.

Clear the area

Move anything off the floor near drains and out of the basement. Keep kids and pets away from standing sewage.

Don't keep flushing

A toilet that won’t clear won’t fix itself on the third try — it just overflows. Leave it.

Don't pour chemicals

Drain chemicals and boiling water won’t move a real blockage and make the line dangerous for us to work on.
