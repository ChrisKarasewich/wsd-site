Do you need us?

When to call

Not every slow drain needs a plumber today, but some do. A sink that drains slow once a month after a shave is usually hair you can pull with a $4 zip tool from the hardware store. Try that first.

The drains that warrant a same-day call: a tub holding water when you shower, a kitchen sink backing up when the dishwasher runs, a laundry standpipe overflowing onto the floor, a basement floor drain gurgling when other fixtures run, more than one drain slow at the same time.

The last one is the big tell. Multiple slow fixtures usually means the problem isn't in any one drain. It's in the sewer line downstream of all of them. That's a different job, different equipment, different page.

If more than one drain is slow, the issue is probably in your sewer line.

Asked a lot

Five things people ask first.

See full FAQ
Do you give quotes over the phone?
Yes, we do! We provide a quote based on the issue you describe, which includes our dispatch fee. But sometimes, there are bigger issues that need to be addressed. In those cases, our technician will provide a quote on site for additional services should they be required.
Do you charge a flat fee to come out?
Yes. A flat dispatch fee covers a sewer and drain tech, a truck, and a real on-site diagnosis.
Do you always run a camera?
No. Most drain cleaning jobs don't need a camera. We run a camera when the line is telling us something, when there are recurring backups, we suspect a break in the line, when there may root intrusion, or before excavation. It's quoted separately.
Do you handle frozen sewer lines?
Yes. We run a steam-thaw setup that's safe on clay tile, cast iron, and PVC. Most thaws take one to three hours.
Will you tear up my yard?
Almost never. We jet, cable, or camera through the existing cleanout 95% of the time. Spot dig is a last resort, and we'll show you the camera footage that proves we need to.
