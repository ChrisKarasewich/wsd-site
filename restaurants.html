Off-hours scheduling

We work the hours you don't.

A kitchen line backs up at 6 pm Friday because nobody serviced it at 3 am Tuesday. Scheduled accounts get a standing service window in your quietest hours — overnight or between meals — so the work happens before it has to.

Standard window — 2 am to 5 am

Most restaurant accounts use this. Two-tech crew, quiet truck, doors locked behind us, key returned. Kitchen ready for prep at 6.

Mid-day window — 2 pm to 4:30 pm

For dinner-only spots that close after lunch. We come in, the kitchen's already broken down from lunch service, we jet and leave before line-up at 5.

Sunday/Monday days

For closed-on-Monday restaurants — daytime work, no rush, less expensive to break out a heavier truck for a chain-flail descale if needed.

Whatever window you pick, it stays your window for the year — same time, same crew, no re-booking. Holidays and Manitoba long weekends are handled by sliding the next visit forward or back by a few days. You approve the calendar once and forget it.

Why every 90 days

Grease doesn't clog a line at once. It does it on a schedule.

A 4-inch kitchen main coats up at a predictable rate — roughly 0.1 inches of effective diameter every 8–10 service days. Most kitchens lose half their flow capacity by day 90. The fix is to reset the clock before that happens.

What we get called for

The five lines that fail first in a commercial kitchen.

41%

Kitchen main grease.

4-inch line from the kitchen out to the grease trap. Coats every time the fryer's drained. Jet quarterly.

23%

Dish pit branch.

Soap scum, food solids, broken glass shards. Standing water at the dish-pit drain by 9 pm Saturday.

14%

Grease trap outlet.

Trap is full or the outlet baffle is fouled. Backs into the kitchen sink first. Not the trap pumper's job — ours.

13%

Mop sink & floor drains.

Mop water + food bits + neglect. Smell first, then a slow drain, then the inspector notices on a Wednesday.

9%

Washroom backup.

Shared lateral with the kitchen — when the kitchen line plugs, the guest washroom shows it first. Camera tells us which.

Restaurant FAQ

Questions operators ask before they sign on.

Do you pump grease traps?
No. Grease-trap pumping is a different service — typically done by a vac truck on a separate schedule. We jet the line going into the trap and the line going out, and we coordinate with whoever you use for pumping so we're not all in the kitchen on the same night. If you don't have a pumper, we'll recommend two we work alongside.
Can you actually work overnight?
Yes — most of our restaurant work happens between 2 am and 5 am. Two-tech crew, quiet truck, in and out before breakfast prep starts at 6. We do dinner-only spots between 2 pm and 4:30 pm. Tell us your service hours and we'll pick a window that doesn't touch them.
What does Manitoba Health ask to see?
Records of FOG (fats-oils-grease) line maintenance — typically a service log showing the date, what was cleaned, and who did it. Our quarterly FOG service sticker on the wall above the grease trap handles the field-inspection version. We send the signed PDF version on request, formatted to match the City of Winnipeg's FOG bylaw paperwork.
How do you bill — per visit, per month, per quarter?
Per quarter. One invoice covers the scheduled visit plus any emergency calls inside that quarter. EFT or credit card. Multi-location accounts get a consolidated monthly statement broken down by restaurant. NET 30 standard.
What if the line backs up between scheduled visits?
Call us. Scheduled accounts get 90-minute priority response, 24/7, with no service-call fee and no after-hours surcharge. Whatever it takes to get you serving again. If it happens twice in one quarter, we'll re-jet for free and re-evaluate the schedule — usually the cycle\u2019s too long for that kitchen.
We have a franchisor that wants documentation. Can you handle that?
Yes. We can send copies of every service event directly to the franchise office on a monthly cadence, in whatever format they specify — PDF, CSV, or upload to a portal. We handle that for two Manitoba franchise groups already; ask the dispatcher and we'll match the format.
Will jetting hurt our older cast iron?
No, on properly-installed cast iron. We camera every line before the nozzle goes in. If the camera shows a crack, a broken hub joint, or paper-thin pipe (rare, but it happens on 1960s installs), we stop and show you on screen — and we\u2019ll quote a spot repair or recommend re-piping that section. We don\u2019t blow pipes apart for sport.
Can you do work outside of sewer and drain — fix a faucet, install a fixture?
No. Sewer and drain only. If you need general plumbing, electrical, or hood-cleaning, we\u2019ll point you at one or two shops we trust. We\u2019d rather refer than half-do something outside our lane.
Do you do walk-in coolers or floor sinks?
Floor sinks, yes — they\u2019re on the same drain system and they\u2019re a magnet for soft buildup. Walk-in cooler drain lines, yes if they tie into the sanitary main. Stand-alone condensate lines that drain to a hub or to grade, no — that\u2019s a refrigeration tech\u2019s call.
