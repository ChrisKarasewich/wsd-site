Flat quotes vs. hourly: why we changed our pricing in 2024.

Two years ago we switched every residential job to a flat quote written before any tool moves. Here's what that did to our average ticket — and to our callbacks.

DW
D. Wiebe
Owner · Winnipeg Sewer & Drain

Two years ago we billed every residential job by the hour, like most of the shops in town. A drain clearing was $185 for the first hour and $145 for every hour after. A sewer cabling was $215 the first hour, same rate after. A quote was a range — usually two-to-four hours, "depending on what we find."

At the start of 2024 we threw the whole thing out. Every residential job now gets a flat quote, written before any tool leaves the truck. If we say it's $295 to clear a kitchen line, it's $295 — whether it takes us twenty minutes or three hours. The number doesn't move once work begins.

Here's what happened to the numbers.

The numbers, before and after.

We pulled two clean comparison samples — 800 residential jobs from the year before the change, 800 from the year after, same service mix, same neighbourhoods.

  • Average ticket: Down 6%. Hourly billed a little high on the easy jobs because techs rounded up. Flat quotes don't.
  • Callbacks (same line, same problem, inside 30 days): Down 41%. This was the surprising one — and the reason we don't regret the change.
  • Quotes that landed within $0 of the final invoice: 100%, vs. 38% under hourly. Worth saying out loud.
  • Average time on-site per job: Down about 14 minutes. Techs stopped padding to make the hour.
  • 5-star Google reviews mentioning "price": Up roughly 3x.

Why callbacks dropped.

This was the part we didn't predict. We thought a flat-quote model would mean techs cut corners — get in, get out, bill the same as if they'd done it right. The opposite happened.

Under hourly, a tech who finished a job in 25 minutes had a problem: he'd just billed someone $185 for less than half an hour. So he'd find something to do — run a second pass, recable a different fixture, "make sure" the line was clean. Most of that work was fine. Some of it was over-jetting that scoured the inside of older clay tile and got us called back two weeks later when a joint started leaking.

Under flat quotes, the tech has no incentive to pad time. He runs the job to the standard we agreed on, confirms it on camera, and leaves. Faster, less stress on the line, fewer callbacks.

What we still bill hourly
Commercial accounts that don't fit a standard service: we'll bill by the hour, but we tell you the cap before we start, and we don't bill past it without checking in. Roughly 5% of our work falls in this bucket — long restoration jobs, multi-day diagnostics on weird systems.

Why the average ticket dropped.

We expected this and priced for it. When you commit to a flat number, you start asking yourself a different question — not "how long might this take?" but "what's a fair price for clearing a kitchen line?" The first question has a fudge factor in it. The second one doesn't.

On the easy 60% of jobs — straightforward drain clears, no surprises — the flat number is genuinely a bit lower than the average hourly invoice for the same work. On the hard 10% — sewer mains with three failure points, frozen lines at 70 feet, jobs where someone's already poured chemical down the line — we lose money on the flat. We've made peace with that. It evens out across the year, and the customer never gets the panic call where the quote doubled while we were under the basement.

What it means for you.

Every WSD residential quote, starting January 2024, looks the same:

  • One line for the work to be done.
  • One flat number.
  • One line saying what triggers a re-quote (essentially: if we find an actual repair needed, not just a clear).
  • Tech signature, your signature, before we touch a tool.

That's it. No surprise invoices. No "the guy was here for four hours instead of two." If you've had a bad experience with a trade's hourly bill before, this is the part of our process that exists specifically because of that.

Book one through the contact page, or call the shop. Ask for the flat quote up front — we'll quote you over the phone if it's a job we can scope by description, and on-site with the camera if it isn't.
