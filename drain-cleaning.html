When you need it

Signs you've got a drain, not a sewer, problem.

Drain problems show up at one fixture at a time. A single sink that's slow. A tub that won't drain. A floor drain that gurgles when the laundry runs but the toilet's fine.

When multiple fixtures back up at once, especially on the lowest floor of the house, that's a sewer line problem, not a drain problem. The job is different and so is the equipment.

Method

What we use depends on what's in the line.

Cable

Soft blockages get a cable.

Hair, soap, food, wet wipes. A motorized cable cuts through it and pulls debris back to the cleanout.

Hydro-jet

Greasy or scaled lines get jetted.

Cable punches a hole through grease. Jet water actually scours the pipe wall, so the next clog is months away, not weeks.

Recurring clogs

Stubborn or recurring clogs get a camera.

If a drain keeps backing up, there's usually a reason inside the pipe that a cable won't fix. We look before we cable.

What we actually find down there

Five things we pull out of Winnipeg sewer lines every week.

01

Roots in clay tile.

Older Winnipeg neighborhoods (Wolseley, the West End, Crescentwood, parts of North End and St. Boniface) sit on clay tile sewer pipe. Joints leak a little water. Roots find them. Over a decade they grow inside the line and choke it.

02

Grease and scale.

Decades of cooking grease, soap, and mineral buildup narrowing a 4-inch pipe to less than 2 inches of clear opening. Common in older kitchens and in any building with a long lateral.

03

Broken pipe.

Clay tile cracks. Cast iron rusts through from the inside. PVC gets crushed when soil shifts. Once the pipe is broken, water leaves the line and debris collects in the gap.

04

Bellies.

The pipe sags from settling soil. Water pools in the low spot. Debris settles into the standing water. Cable a belly all you want; it'll be back next quarter. The camera locates it and the repair is a spot dig.

05

Frozen line.

Winnipeg clay tile under shallow yards and driveway crossings freezes January through March when standing water sits in the line overnight. That's a different page.

Drain cleaning FAQ

Questions on this service.

My drain is slow but not totally blocked. Should I wait?
For Winnipeg homes on clay tile with mature trees in the yard, once a year is normal. For newer PVC lines without root pressure, only as needed.
My basement has flooded, what do I do first?
Power off any submerged fixtures or outlets if it's safe to do so. Then call us. We'll give you a quote over the phone, then send a plumber to your home — and you'll have it in writing before any work starts.
Do you clean main drains and floor drains?
Yes. Floor drains are common in older Winnipeg basements and they back up first when there's a sewer line problem upstream.
How long does a typical drain cleaning take?
30 minutes to two hours, depending on the line and the cause.
Will I need a permit?
Not for cleaning or camera work. Spot repairs and full sewer replacement usually require a City of Winnipeg permit, which we pull as part of the quote.
Will hydro jetting damage my pipe?
Modern PVC and cast iron handle jetting fine. Older clay tile or already-cracked pipe can be vulnerable, which is why we run a camera before jetting. If the line isn't a candidate for jetting, we cable it.
