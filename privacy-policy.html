Privacy Policy

1. Overview

Winnipeg Sewer & Drain ("WSD," "we," "us," or "our") is a sewer and drain service company operating in Winnipeg, Manitoba. We respect your privacy and handle your personal information in accordance with Canada's Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA).

This policy explains what we collect when you visit our website, call our booking line, or hire us for a job, and what we do with it. By using our website or services, you agree to the practices described here.

2. Information we collect

We only collect what we need to quote your job, get a truck to your address, and follow up afterward. That generally means:

Information you give us

  • Contact details — your name, phone number, email address, and the service address where you need work done.
  • Job details — what's wrong (a backed-up sewer, a slow drain), photos or videos you choose to send, and any access notes.
  • Booking & payment records — appointment times, invoices, and the method of payment. We do not store full credit card numbers; card payments are processed by our payment provider.
  • Messages — anything you write in our contact form, by text, or by email.

Information collected automatically

  • Device & usage data — your browser type, approximate location (city-level), pages viewed, and the link that referred you, gathered through standard analytics.
  • Camera-inspection footage — where you book a sewer camera inspection, we record video of your line as part of the service and provide you a copy.

3. How we use your information

We use your information to:

  • Prepare quotes, schedule visits, and dispatch a technician to your address;
  • Carry out the work and provide inspection reports, footage, and invoices;
  • Contact you about your appointment, follow up after a job, and handle warranty callbacks;
  • Respond to your questions, reviews, and requests;
  • Improve our website and service area coverage; and
  • Meet legal, tax, and insurance obligations.

We do not sell your personal information, and we don't send marketing texts or emails unless you've asked us to. If you ever do opt in, every message includes a way to opt back out.

4. Cookies & analytics

Our website uses a small number of cookies and similar technologies to remember your preferences and understand how the site is used so we can make it better. We use privacy-respecting analytics to count visits and see which service pages are most useful.

You can block or delete cookies through your browser settings. The site will still work — you may just lose conveniences like remembered form entries.

5. Sharing & disclosure

We keep your information within our team and share it only when it's needed to do the job or required by law. Specifically, we may share information with:

  • Service providers who work on our behalf — for example, our scheduling software, payment processor, and analytics provider — and only to the extent they need it;
  • Property managers or landlords where you've engaged us through them, or them through you, for a shared property;
  • Authorities or insurers when required by law, court order, or to establish, defend, or settle a legal claim.

Where a provider operates outside Canada, your information may be processed in another country and become subject to that country's laws.

6. How long we keep it

We keep your information only as long as we need it for the purposes above, or as long as the law requires. Job records, invoices, and inspection footage are typically retained for up to seven years to meet tax, warranty, and insurance requirements. Website analytics data is kept for a shorter period in aggregate form.

7. How we protect it

We use reasonable physical, technical, and organizational safeguards to protect your information against loss, theft, and unauthorized access — including encrypted connections on our website and access controls on our booking system. No method of transmission or storage is ever completely secure, but we take your trust seriously and act quickly if something goes wrong.

8. Your rights

Under PIPEDA, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you;
  • Correct information that is inaccurate or out of date;
  • Withdraw consent to our use of your information, subject to legal and contractual limits; and
  • Ask questions or make a complaint about how we handle your information.

To make a request, contact us using the details below. We'll respond within a reasonable time, usually within 30 days. If you're not satisfied with our response, you may contact the Office of the Privacy Commissioner of Canada.

9. Children's privacy

Our services are intended for property owners, tenants, and managers — not children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 16. If you believe a child has provided us information, please contact us and we'll delete it.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time as our services or the law change. When we do, we'll revise the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Significant changes will be highlighted on our website. Please check back periodically.
