General

The questions Winnipeg homeowners ask us first.

From the homepage →
Do you give quotes over the phone?
Yes, we do! We provide a quote based on the issue you describe, which includes our dispatch fee. But sometimes, there are bigger issues that need to be addressed. In those cases, our technician will provide a quote on site for additional services should they be required.
Do you charge a flat fee to come out?
Yes. A flat dispatch fee covers a sewer and drain tech, a truck, and a real on-site diagnosis.
Do you always run a camera?
No. Most drain cleaning jobs don't need a camera. We run a camera when the line is telling us something, when there are recurring backups, we suspect a break in the line, when there may root intrusion, or before excavation. It's quoted separately.
Do you handle frozen sewer lines?
Yes. We run a steam-thaw setup that's safe on clay tile, cast iron, and PVC. Most thaws take one to three hours.
Will you tear up my yard?
Almost never. We jet, cable, or camera through the existing cleanout 95% of the time. Spot dig is a last resort, and we'll show you the camera footage that proves we need to.

Drain cleaning

Homeowners with a slow or blocked drain.

View the drain cleaning page →
My drain is slow but not totally blocked. Should I wait?
For Winnipeg homes on clay tile with mature trees in the yard, once a year is normal. For newer PVC lines without root pressure, only as needed.
My basement has flooded, what do I do first?
Power off any submerged fixtures or outlets if it's safe to do so. Then call us. We'll give you a quote over the phone, then send a plumber to your home — and you'll have it in writing before any work starts.
Do you clean main drains and floor drains?
Yes. Floor drains are common in older Winnipeg basements and they back up first when there's a sewer line problem upstream.
How long does a typical drain cleaning take?
30 minutes to two hours, depending on the line and the cause.
Will I need a permit?
Not for cleaning or camera work. Spot repairs and full sewer replacement usually require a City of Winnipeg permit, which we pull as part of the quote.
Will hydro jetting damage my pipe?
Modern PVC and cast iron handle jetting fine. Older clay tile or already-cracked pipe can be vulnerable, which is why we run a camera before jetting. If the line isn't a candidate for jetting, we cable it.

Sewer line cleaning

When the whole house backs up at the main lateral.

View the sewer line cleaning page →
My toilet, tub, and floor drain all backed up at once. What's going on?
That's a textbook lateral restriction. Everything in the house drains through one pipe to the city main; when that pipe is choked, the lowest fixtures back up first. Don't run more water — call us and we'll camera the line and clear it.
Do I own the lateral or does the city?
In Winnipeg, the homeowner owns the lateral from the house all the way to the city main connection — including the section under the boulevard and street. If the problem is past the main, that's on the city. Our camera tells you exactly where the issue sits.
How often should a sewer line be cleaned?
If you've got mature trees over a clay tile lateral — every 12–24 months as preventative. Newer PVC laterals with no root pressure may only need a clean when something changes. We'll tell you on the first visit which camp you're in.
Will cabling damage clay tile?
Modern sectional cable with the right head is safe on intact clay tile. The risk is when the tile is already broken — cable can catch the offset and worsen it. That's why we run a camera before cabling. If the line is compromised, we jet at lower pressure or skip straight to a repair plan.
What if the camera finds broken pipe?
We stop and show you the footage on screen. You'll get three written options — spot repair, trenchless liner, or full replacement — with prices, before anyone digs. The $295 camera fee applies to whichever repair you choose.
Can you clear the line through a toilet or sink instead of a cleanout?
We can, but we don't if we can help it. A proper main cleanout gives us straight-line access to the lateral. Pulling a toilet adds time and a reset fee. If you don't have a cleanout, we'll quote installing one — it's worth it the second time the line clogs.
How long does a typical sewer line cleaning take?
Camera plus cable, one to three hours. Camera plus jet, two to four hours. If we find broken pipe, the cleaning stops and the conversation turns to repair planning.

Hydro jetting

High-pressure cleaning for grease, scale, and roots.

View the hydro jetting page →
Won't 4,000 PSI of water blow my pipe apart?
No — and the camera makes sure of it. Modern PVC, cast iron, and ABS handle 4,000 PSI without issue. Older clay tile and Orangeburg can be vulnerable if it's already cracked, which is exactly what the camera tells us before the nozzle goes in. If the line isn't a jetting candidate, we tell you and use a different method.
How is jetting different from cabling or snaking?
A cable cuts a channel through a clog. Water keeps flowing, but the pipe wall is still coated with grease, scale, and sediment — so the next clog forms quickly. Jetting strips the pipe wall back to its original diameter. For a clog you've cleared three times in a year, jet it once and you usually get a year or more before the next one.
How long does a typical jetting job take?
Camera plus jet on a 50–80 foot residential line runs about 90 minutes to 2.5 hours. Restaurant kitchen mains usually run two to four hours depending on length and grease severity.
Does the jet make a mess?
Done right, no. We jet from a downstream cleanout so debris flushes back the way the nozzle came in — into the cleanout, where we vacuum it out. We lay tarps on hard flooring and bring shoe covers. The truck handles the wastewater; nothing goes into your yard.
Will jetting fix a broken pipe?
No. If the camera shows broken, offset, or collapsed pipe, jetting won't help — and high-pressure water through a hole will wash soil into the cavity and make repairs worse. We stop, show you the footage, and quote a spot repair or liner instead. Your camera fee applies to the repair.
How often should a line be jetted?
Residential kitchen lines with heavy use: every 18–24 months. Cast iron mains over 50 years old: every 12–24 months. Restaurant kitchen mains: every 3–6 months. We tell you a recommended interval after the first job, based on what the camera shows.
Do I need a cleanout for you to jet?
Strongly preferred. A proper main cleanout gives us straight-line access and contains debris flush-back. If you don't have one, we can sometimes jet through a roof vent or pulled toilet, but it's slower and there's a reset fee. We can quote installing a cleanout — usually pays for itself the second time the line needs work.
Can hydro jetting damage tree roots in the line permanently?
It cuts soft roots cleanly, but the roots themselves are growing outside the pipe and will come back. The lasting fix is either a root-control foam treatment after the jet (slows regrowth by 1–3 years), or fixing the joint the roots came through — typically a spot repair or pipe liner. We'll lay out the options after the camera.

Sewer camera inspection

Seeing exactly what is happening in the line.

View the sewer camera inspection page →
How long does a camera inspection take?
A residential lateral — house to city main — takes about 45 minutes to 90 minutes start to finish, including setup, the camera run, sonde location of any findings, and walking you through the footage on a tablet before we leave. Mini push-rod inspections on a single branch line are faster — usually under an hour.
Do you need a cleanout? What if I don't have one?
A proper main cleanout is the best entry point — it gives the camera straight-line access and saves time. If you don't have one, we can sometimes go through a pulled toilet or a roof vent stack, but it's slower and a bit messier. We'll tell you on the phone whether your house likely has a cleanout based on the era it was built. If you don't have one, we can quote installing one — it pays for itself the next time anything goes wrong.
Will the camera fit in my line?
The mainline camera fits any pipe 3 inches or larger — which covers every residential sewer lateral and most building drains in Winnipeg. For smaller branch lines (kitchen sinks, lavatories, washing machine standpipes) we switch to a mini push-rod camera that goes down to 1.5 inch. The only lines we can't scope are pressurized water supply lines (different game) and 1 1/4″ trap arms.
What if the camera finds nothing wrong?
Great — that's the best outcome of a $295 inspection. You get the footage, a one-page report saying the line is sound, and a recommended next-scope date (usually 5 to 7 years out for an older Winnipeg property). Lots of our jobs end this way. We don't invent problems.
Can I get a copy of the footage for an insurance claim?
Yes — every camera job comes with the full video file, emailed to you the same day, plus a written one-page report with timestamps. Insurance adjusters and city public-works departments accept it. We've also been called as a witness in a couple of disputes; if you need formal documentation beyond the standard report, just ask.
Why is camera inspection so important for older Winnipeg homes?
A lot of homes built before about 1970 still have clay tile or Orangeburg laterals between the house and the city main. Both materials fail predictably with age — roots get in through joint seals, the pipe sags or collapses, soil washes in. The footage is the only reliable way to know whether you've got 20 years left on the line or two.
Does the camera fee really roll into the repair if I book one?
Yes. If we camera the line, find a problem, and you book us to fix it within 30 days, the $295 comes off the repair invoice. If the camera is the whole job — insurance documentation, peace of mind on an older line — the $295 is what you pay, flat.

Frozen sewer thawing

Winnipeg winters and steam-thaw service.

View the frozen sewer thawing page →
How do I know it's actually frozen, and not just a clog?
Two signs together: every drain in the house is dead at once (a regular clog is usually one branch line), and a cable run can't get past the property-line area — it hits something hard and slick and slides back. Time of year matters too. If it's January, the house had a cold night, and you've got both symptoms, it's a freeze. We'll confirm on site before charging.
How long does a thaw take?
Most residential thaws are between one and three hours from set-up to clear-flow. A short freeze (10 to 20 feet inside the foundation) can be done in under an hour. A long deep freeze that runs all the way to the city main can be four to six hours in extreme cases. We'll give you a time estimate as soon as we know how far the plug is, usually within the first 15 minutes.
Is steam thawing safe on my pipe?
Yes. Steam at 212°F is the same temperature as boiling water, just delivered through an insulated rod inside the pipe. It's safe on clay tile, cast iron, PVC, ABS, lead, concrete, and Orangeburg. We've never thawed a line we damaged. The dangerous methods — electric arc and open flame — we don't run.
Will my line freeze again next winter?
If the reason it froze is just an unusually cold night and standing water in a normally fine line, probably not — but it depends on the cause. About 60% of the frozen lines we thaw have an underlying problem (a belly, a partial root mass, a low-pitched section). That's why we recommend a same-day camera scope at half price — so you know whether to expect this every January or it was a one-off.
Can the City of Winnipeg help cover the cost?
The City runs a frozen-water-service program in extreme-cold winters that covers frozen incoming water supply lines, not frozen sewers. Frozen sanitary sewers between your house and the city main are the homeowner's responsibility. That said, ask your insurance — some Manitoba sewer-backup policies cover thaw costs after a backup incident. We provide an itemized invoice for the claim.
What about the city main? Whose side is the freeze on?
The dividing line is the property line — sometimes the curb, sometimes a foot or two onto the boulevard. From your basement out to that line is your responsibility. From there to the city main is the City's. If we thaw and the rod goes 90 feet without finding water, it usually means the freeze is past your property line and we'll help you open a ticket with 311 — they roll their own crew.
Can I get a thaw at 11 p.m.?
Yes. We run an after-hours line. Frozen sewer with a houseful of people is the kind of call we open the bay door for at midnight. There's no after-hours surcharge — the rate is the same as a Tuesday afternoon. If we're already on another emergency, we'll give you a real ETA and tell you what to do in the meantime.
Do you handle frozen drains inside the building, like a floor drain or laundry standpipe?
Yes — those are usually faster jobs. A standpipe or floor drain that froze near a poorly-insulated wall is often thawed in 20 to 40 minutes with a portable steamer or a hot-water flush. Same flat starting rate, same no-after-hours-surcharge policy.

Restaurants & commercial kitchens

FOG maintenance, overnight service, and documentation.

View the restaurants & commercial kitchens page →
Do you pump grease traps?
No. Grease-trap pumping is a different service — typically done by a vac truck on a separate schedule. We jet the line going into the trap and the line going out, and we coordinate with whoever you use for pumping so we're not all in the kitchen on the same night. If you don't have a pumper, we'll recommend two we work alongside.
Can you actually work overnight?
Yes — most of our restaurant work happens between 2 am and 5 am. Two-tech crew, quiet truck, in and out before breakfast prep starts at 6. We do dinner-only spots between 2 pm and 4:30 pm. Tell us your service hours and we'll pick a window that doesn't touch them.
What does Manitoba Health ask to see?
Records of FOG (fats-oils-grease) line maintenance — typically a service log showing the date, what was cleaned, and who did it. Our quarterly FOG service sticker on the wall above the grease trap handles the field-inspection version. We send the signed PDF version on request, formatted to match the City of Winnipeg's FOG bylaw paperwork.
How do you bill — per visit, per month, per quarter?
Per quarter. One invoice covers the scheduled visit plus any emergency calls inside that quarter. EFT or credit card. Multi-location accounts get a consolidated monthly statement broken down by restaurant. NET 30 standard.
What if the line backs up between scheduled visits?
Call us. Scheduled accounts get 90-minute priority response, 24/7, with no service-call fee and no after-hours surcharge. Whatever it takes to get you serving again. If it happens twice in one quarter, we'll re-jet for free and re-evaluate the schedule — usually the cycle’s too long for that kitchen.
We have a franchisor that wants documentation. Can you handle that?
Yes. We can send copies of every service event directly to the franchise office on a monthly cadence, in whatever format they specify — PDF, CSV, or upload to a portal. We handle that for two Manitoba franchise groups already; ask the dispatcher and we'll match the format.
Will jetting hurt our older cast iron?
No, on properly-installed cast iron. We camera every line before the nozzle goes in. If the camera shows a crack, a broken hub joint, or paper-thin pipe (rare, but it happens on 1960s installs), we stop and show you on screen — and we’ll quote a spot repair or recommend re-piping that section. We don’t blow pipes apart for sport.
Can you do work outside of sewer and drain — fix a faucet, install a fixture?
No. Sewer and drain only. If you need general plumbing, electrical, or hood-cleaning, we’ll point you at one or two shops we trust. We’d rather refer than half-do something outside our lane.
Do you do walk-in coolers or floor sinks?
Floor sinks, yes — they’re on the same drain system and they’re a magnet for soft buildup. Walk-in cooler drain lines, yes if they tie into the sanitary main. Stand-alone condensate lines that drain to a hub or to grade, no — that’s a refrigeration tech’s call.

Property managers

SLAs, billing, insurance, and tenant access.

View the property managers page →
Do we have to sign a contract?
No. There's a one-page agreement that confirms the SLA tiers, who's authorized to open calls on each building, and the billing terms (NET 30 standard). It's open-ended — either side can end the arrangement with 30 days' notice. We don't sell prepaid hours and we won't lock you into a multi-year deal.
How do you bill — per building, per event, per month?
Per event. One monthly statement aggregates every service event across your portfolio, line-itemized by building and unit. EFT or credit card. We can also bill the corporation, the management company, or the unit owner directly — set per building during onboarding.
Can the tenant call you directly?
Yes — if you set it up that way. Each building has a list of authorized callers (manager, resident manager, sometimes tenants for emergencies only). When the tenant calls, dispatch confirms the building, opens a ticket against your account, and copies you on the work order within an hour. No surprises on the monthly statement.
What happens after-hours? Is there a different number?
Same number. (204) 786-4060 rolls to the on-call dispatcher between 11 pm and 7 am. P1 emergency calls are dispatched immediately, no surcharge. P2 calls are confirmed for first thing the next morning with a phone call (not a voicemail) by 8 am.
Do you carry insurance? Can you name us as additional insured?
Yes. We carry $5M commercial general liability, $2M auto, and WCB Manitoba coverage. We can name your management company or any specific corporation as additional insured on the COI. Send the request through your dispatcher and we'll issue it within two business days.
What if you can't make the SLA?
The call-out fee for that visit is waived. We also send a written note to the building file explaining what happened. We've missed the P1 SLA three times in 2025 — twice during the April thaw flood, once during a wind storm that closed half the city. We don't make a habit of it.
Will your tech show ID and respect tenant access?
Yes. Photo ID, WSD-branded uniform, and a 'today's tech' email sent to the manager before the visit with name and photo. We knock before entering a unit even with manager-provided keys, and we don't enter occupied units unless a tenant is home or you've explicitly authorized entry.
Can you do work outside of sewer and drain — e.g. fix a leaking faucet while you're there?
No. Sewer and drain only. If you need general plumbing we'll recommend two shops we've worked alongside on shared jobs. We'd rather refer than half-do something outside our lane.
What about condo corporations — who signs?
The corporation does, normally through the property manager acting on the board's behalf. We're happy to attend one board meeting during onboarding to walk through the SLA and the drain-map process if it helps the board sign off.
