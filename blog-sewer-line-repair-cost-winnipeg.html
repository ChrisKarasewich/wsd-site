How much does sewer line repair cost in Winnipeg? (2026 guide.)

Sewer repair covers everything from a quick cleaning to digging up the front yard. Here is how the pricing actually works, what moves the number, and how to make sure you are not overpaying.

DW
D. Wiebe
Owner · Winnipeg Sewer & Drain

If you've called around for a sewer line repair quote in Winnipeg, you've probably heard a wide range of numbers. That's because "sewer line repair" covers everything from a quick cleaning to digging up the front yard.

Here's how the pricing actually works, what the numbers mean, and how to make sure you're not getting overcharged.

The short version
Sewer repair in Winnipeg runs from a small charge for a single-spot fix up to several thousand for a full line replacement. The price depends on the length of the line, how deep it is, what it's made of, and what kind of repair the line actually needs. The only honest quote is one based on a camera inspection.

What "sewer line repair" actually includes

Five different jobs get bundled under that name, cheapest to most expensive:

  • Drain cleaning. Clearing a clog. The cheapest end.
  • Hydro jetting. Cleaning the full inside of the pipe with high-pressure water.
  • Camera inspection. A camera run through the line.
  • Spot repair. Fixing a single damaged section.
  • Full line replacement. Replacing the entire line from house to property line, or further.

Knowing which one you need is half the battle. A camera inspection tells you.

What moves the cost

Five factors move the price more than anything else.

  • Length of the damaged section. A 4-foot crack costs less than a 30-foot collapse. The camera shows exactly how much pipe is affected.
  • Depth of the line. Winnipeg sewer lines run anywhere from 6 to 14 feet deep. Deeper lines need more excavation, and more excavation costs more.
  • Pipe material. Clay tile, cast iron, concrete, ABS, PVC — each has different failure modes and repair approaches.
  • Location. A line under a driveway, a deck, or a mature tree costs more to access than one through open lawn.
  • Method. Traditional excavation versus trenchless (relining or pipe bursting). Trenchless costs more per foot but avoids tearing up your yard, driveway, or sidewalk.

Spot repair vs. full replacement

Spot repair fixes a single damaged section. It's the right call when the rest of the line is healthy. Full replacement makes sense when the line has multiple failure points, or when the material is at the end of its life — most clay tile from before 1960, most cast iron from before 1980.

A camera inspection makes this call for you. You see what's wrong, where, and how bad. You decide what you want to spend.

How to avoid getting overcharged

  • Get a camera inspection before any quote. Without a camera in the line, every quote is a guess — and a guess is how you end up paying for work you didn't need.
  • Get the quote in writing. The number on the page is the number you pay. WSD provides flat quotes backed by camera evidence before any work starts.
  • Ask what's included. Excavation, permits, restoration of your lawn or driveway, disposal of old pipe. Make sure the cheap quote actually covers them.

Frequently asked questions

Does homeowner's insurance cover sewer line repair?

Sometimes. Standard policies usually don't cover damage from regular wear or root intrusion, but some have a sewer-line endorsement that does. Check with your provider. Damage caused by a city-side issue may be the city's responsibility.

Who is responsible for the sewer line in Winnipeg — the homeowner or the city?

The homeowner owns the section from inside the house to the property line. The city owns it from the property line to the main under the street. Most repair jobs are on the homeowner side.

Is trenchless sewer repair available in Winnipeg?

Yes, for many situations. Pipe relining and pipe bursting can avoid the dig where the line isn't fully collapsed. The camera inspection determines whether trenchless is an option.

How long does a sewer line repair take?

A spot repair is usually a one-day job. A full replacement takes two to four days, depending on length, depth, and conditions. Trenchless work is faster than traditional excavation in most cases.

Do you need a permit for sewer line repair in Winnipeg?

Yes, in most cases. A licensed contractor handles the permit as part of the job. WSD includes permits in the flat quote when one is required.
